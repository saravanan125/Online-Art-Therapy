The Healing Power of Art Therapy in Digital Spaces

Dr. Sarah Jenkin
October 15, 2025
5 min read

One of the most common things I hear from new clients is, "But I can't even draw a stick figure." There is a deep-seated belief that to engage in art making, one must be "good" at it.

Art therapy challenges this notion entirely.

Process Over Product

In our studio, we focus on the process of creating, not the final product. The act of choosing a color that matches your mood, or making a chaotic scribble to release anger, is where the healing happens. The aesthetic value of the final piece is irrelevant.

"Art washes away from the soul the dust of everyday life." â€” Pablo Picasso

Your Inner Critic

Art therapy provides a safe space to confront your inner critic. That voice that says "this looks ugly" or "you're doing it wrong" is often the same voice that fuels anxiety and self-doubt in other areas of your life. By learning to create without judgment, you practice self-compassion.

Getting Started

If you're hesitant, start small.

  • Doodle while on the phone.
  • Use a coloring book.
  • Play with playdough.

Remember, there is no "right" way to do art therapy. There is only your way.
